Introduction to Programming

What is programming?

Writing human "readable" text that is transformed into a series of commands a computer can understand

  • Flesh out the "readable" part by describing what different languages are

What kind of tasks would make good a candidate for a program?

  • Repeatable tasks
  • Manual tasks with risks of human error
  • Pulling together lots of data from a wide range of sources
  • Computers are good at storing and gathering raw data doing processes on it.

What kind of tasks would not make a good candidate for a program?

  • Making decisions
  • Understanding concepts
  • Doing anything with images!!
  • All of these are changing as innovations in machine learning are being made
  • Humans are better then computers at experimental learning, making decisions, seeing connections between data
  • Looking at a picture of a dog and knowing it is a dog!

Computers are very literal

We can help prevent angry computers by taking a writing step by step instructions on what we intend our programs to do

Understanding Psuedocode

  • Planning technique to write out a programs logic
  • Language agnostic
  • Great tool for collaborating

Psuedocode Techniques

A simple list

  1. Boil water in kettle
  2. Put tea bag in cup
  3. Wait for water to boil
  4. Add boiled water to cup
  5. Add sugar
  6. Add milk
  7. Serve
